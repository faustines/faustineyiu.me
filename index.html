Career:

B.S. Computer Science, Technology Management Minor

As someone who has been in the work industry since 14 years old, I've seen many management styles and have held jobs in many different fields. What stood out to me was the power of technology, since it allows great precision in problem-solving without traces of bias, unless the creator prefers otherwise. The possibilities through technology are both incredible and horrifying. Thus, I wish to become a creator with great responsibility in maintaining a better future on my part when I get there.

My career aspiration is to innovate ideas and products that create a new level of sustainable advancement as a
Software Engineer or Developer.

I plan to extend my skills into pursuing humanitarian, environmental, and social justice projects that I am passionate about. At this moment in time, everything is a learning experience for me to find my ultimate purpose in life.

Hobbies

My hobbies are what keep me creative, active, and healthy, both physically and mentally. I believe in maintaining a good balance of fun and work.

Music:

Flute, Drum, Street Dance, Elsie the Turtle Band

Started off with Flute, then listened to modern music, which got me interested in Street Dance, so I listened to my iPod on the daily and paid attention to the beats, which jump-started my Drum skills, started a band in high school, picking up theguitar, and my love for music continues on.

Sports:

Basketball, Hiking, Healthy work outs

Basketball has been my life since 6th grade in middle school. I've always told my mom that there is nothing in life that gave me as much joy as basketball did. It was a learning journey to reach up to Varsity my Junior year in high school. The decision of giving up the team after that year was difficult, but I knew was for the best. I always name basketball my best mistake, because I genuinely love it. It started my routine in outdoor activities and work outs. Basketball continues to remain a stress reliever during Finals and in my general routines.

Poems:

Shel Silverstein was my favorite author when I was in gradeschool. I love anything that rhymes and have written a couple of rhyming nonsense myself. Planning on publishing them as a blog just for fun.

Travel:

Thailand, China, Taiwan, Canada, Europe, and more to come

Thanks to the generous people in my life, I was able to explore different parts of the world since I was really young. I have wondered whether I was spoiled or actually learning something. It wasn't until my Thailand trip in first grade where I published an elementary news article about my experience where I understood the meaning of traveling. The different sides of the world make imprints within me that was significantly astonishing because it was a completely new experience that I would never have seen unless I have gone personally. Sooner or later, every trip I've made unconsciously becomes valuable learning experiences.
